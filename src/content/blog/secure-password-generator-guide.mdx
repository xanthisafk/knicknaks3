## The Hidden Dangers of Online Password Generators

When you search for a **random password generator**, you are met with thousands of websites offering to create a secure key for your new accounts. But here is the catch: how do you know they aren't logging the passwords they generate? If a generator runs server-side (using PHP, Node, Python, etc.), your newly generated password travels over the wire and into a server's memory. It could be logged, recorded, or intercepted.

At Knicknaks, we believe your security tools shouldn't be security risks.

## Why Client-Side Generation is the Only Way

A client-side secure password generator ensures that the password creation logic occurs entirely on your device—inside your browser—using secure mathematical algorithms (like the Web Crypto API's `crypto.getRandomValues()`).

### The Benefits

1. **Zero Data Transmission:** No network requests mean nothing can be intercepted.
2. **True Randomness:** Browsers have deep access to the OS's entropy pool, allowing for highly secure, cryptographically strong random number generation.
3. **Instant Results:** Since it's mathematical and local, it's blazing fast. No loading spinners.

## Characteristics of an Unbreakable Password

Generating the password is just the first step. You need to ensure the parameters you choose actually make the password resistant to modern brute-force techniques.

### Length is King

In 2026, computational power makes short passwords obsolete. A 10-character password can be cracked by specialized server farms in under an hour. Here are the modern recommendations:

- **12 Characters:** Bare minimum for non-critical accounts.
- **16 Characters:** Standard recommendation for email, financial, or primary accounts.
- **20+ Characters:** Highly advised for master passwords (like your password manager vault key).

### Complexity and Character Sets

Relying solely on length isn't enough; you need entropy (randomness). A strong password utilizes all available character sets:

- **Uppercase Letters** (A-Z)
- **Lowercase Letters** (a-z)
- **Numbers** (0-9)
- **Symbols** (!@#$%, etc.)

Our tool provides quick toggles to include or exclude these sets, allowing you to generate passwords that adhere strictly to specific site requirements.

## Best Practices for Account Security

1. **Use a Password Manager:** Don't try to remember your 16-character random strings. Use a localized or zero-knowledge cloud password manager.
2. **Never Reuse Passwords:** A security breach on a minor forum shouldn't compromise your primary email address.
3. **Enable 2FA:** Two-Factor Authentication (especially hardware keys like YubiKey or authenticator apps) is arguably more important than the password itself.
